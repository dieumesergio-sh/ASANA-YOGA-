Practice For Begginers

1.Mountain Pose (Tadasana): A foundational standing pose that improves posture and balance.

2.Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana): A great full-body stretch that strengthens the arms and legs.

3.Child‚Äôs Pose (Balasana): A restful pose that stretches the back and hips.

4.Cat-Cow Pose (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana): A gentle flow between two poses that warms up the spine.

5.Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I): Strengthens the legs and opens the hips and chest.

6.Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II): Builds strength and stamina in the legs and core.

7.Tree Pose (Vrksasana): Improves balance and strengthens the legs.

8.Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana): Opens the chest and strengthens the back and legs.

9.Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana): Stretches the back and hamstrings.

10.Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana): Strengthens the back and opens the chest.

These poses can help you build a strong foundation in yoga. Remember to focus on your breath and move at your own pace. If you want more detailed guidance, you might find video tutorials helpful, such as the ones from Yoga With Adriene12.

Practice For Intermidiate

1.Triangle Pose (Trikonasana): Stretches the sides of the body and improves balance.

2.Chair Pose (Utkatasana): Strengthens the legs and core.

3.Plank Pose: Builds strength in the arms, shoulders, and core.

4.Corpse Pose (Savasana): A relaxation pose to end your practice.

5.Happy Baby Pose (Ananda Balasana): Stretches the inner thighs and lower back.

6.Supine Twist (Supta Matsyendrasana): Releases tension in the spine and shoulders.

7.Butterfly Pose (Baddha Konasana): Opens the hips and stretches the inner thighs.

8.Low Lunge (Anjaneyasana): Stretches the hips and strengthens thelegs.

9.Boat Pose (Navasana): Strengthens the core and improves balance.

10.Legs-Up-The-Wall Pose (Viparita Karani): A restorative pose that relaxes the body and mind.
